\section{Manifold}
We shall first define topological manifold and then provide them with a smooth structure to form smooth manifold. An $n$-dimensional topological manifold is a topological space that is locally modeled by $\R^n$.  \\

\begin{fdefinition}[Topological manifold] Let $n \in \N$. A topological $n$-manifold, $M$, is a \textbf{second countable, Hausdorff} topological space such that every point $p \in M$ has a neighbourhood $N \ni p$ and a homeomorphism $\varphi: N \to U$ where $U \subset \R^n$ is an open set. 
\end{fdefinition}
